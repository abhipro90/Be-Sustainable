Plastic Waste


You use it every day, probably only once and then you most likely throw it away.

What is it?

It's plastic.


It is estimated that 4 million tons of litter gets in the sea…. Every day!

This is like a small mountain of garbage day in day out that gets into our oceans.


Did you ever think of what happens to it?

Most of the litter, especially the plastic takes up to 500 years to disintegrate completely.

That is a lot of time to float around.


In the oceans there are circular currents which carry the floating litter to specific areas like the Great Pacific Garbage Patch.

This is an area in the Northern Pacific that is estimated to be as big as the United States.

Imagine an island of floating garbage as big as the United States!


What’s the problem?


Over time plastic breaks into smaller pieces, which fish and birds mistake for food and eat. So plastic eventually goes up the food chain to us when we eat fish.


Unfortunately plastic contains chemicals which are bad for your body and one of the effects is cancer.


What about solutions?


So far people have tried to do some cleaning with boats and special nets. But the Pacific ocean is humongous and it is estimated that in this way it would take almost 80.000 years and cost a fortune.


One solution to the problem was invented by Boyan Slat, a boy of only 19 years old.

Boyan created the Ocean Cleanup Project and the concept is simple:

  1. Place a floating barrier in the middle of the ocean currents. The barrier has a V shape and is hundreds of meters long
  2. The currents will push the floating litter towards the center of the barrier where there is a machine that captures the plastic and extracts it from the water.
  3. There are no nets so that it does not harm the marine animals.
  4. The Plastic is then stored in a container and can be resold and recycled
  5. The machine works automatically (without people) and every month or so a ship comes to empty the litter that has been collected

So instead of going around the ocean with boats to collect the floating garbage you let the currents bring the litter to you and as the founder Mr. Boyan says: “Why move through the Ocean if the ocean can move through you”.


In conclusion dumping plastic in the sea affects both us and the environment, polluting the sea and killing the animals. In response to this problem a study by Ocean Cleanup project is trying to solve the problem by putting a machine called The Ocean Cleanup Array in the sea. It’s going to start soon in 2020 and expected to clean the Great Pacific Garbage Patch in 20 years instead of 80.000!
